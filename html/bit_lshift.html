int bit.lshift (int input, int count)

Left shifting just moves every bit once to the left, cutting out every bit that runs out of the space on the left. (You always have 4 bytes of room, remember!)

In a way it's a really fast multiplication by two. Watch out though, since if a 1 bit runs into the left-most bit, it'll turn the entire value into a negative value with odd results. 

  1000 0010 
<< 2
= 0000 1000
