int bit.arshift (int input, int count)

Arithmetic right shifting is special, because instead of simple right shifting, it keeps the sign bit in place and moves all other bits to the right instead.

In the case of left shifting, left shifts already keep the sign bit in place because of two's complement rules - negative numbers are represented by a sign bit of 1 and every other bit flipped - meaning another 1 will usually be moved in place anyway.
